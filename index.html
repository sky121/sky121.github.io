A Little About Myself

My name is Skylar Hoffman and I am a Computer Sciene Major at UC Irvine. I love creating and developing unique ideas and solving complex problems. As a programmmer I have always excelled in persistantly tackling bugs and errors without hesistating or giving up. I love the feeling of solving a difficult, challenging issue with a program and viewing the completion and full compilation of its code. I am never just focused on completing a programming assignment, I always push myself to go beyond and beyond to try and solve the problem in the best way possible.


In my freetime, I enjoy many activities such as creating and listening to music. Over the last two summers, I have taught myself how to play guitar and have discovered that by tapping into my creative nature with music, I am able to fully comprehend and allow my feelings and thoughts to flow. Music has always brought out the deepest of emotions in me, and by playing guitar I can truly release my stresses. Another love of mine is bowling. Because of the enthralling competitive nature of bowling, I have always found great joy in pushing myself to new limits. The feeling I get when I score a "strike" is as powerful as the heartracing excitement


released within in me when I press compile and see my vast journey of hardwork pay off. Additionally, I love animals, archery, hiking, nature, and have a passion for dogs. Since my childhood, my parents brought me to beautiful, serene, hiking trails where the smell of freshly sprouting trees and the dew on rain pattered leaves have always inspired me to become a more open minded thinker.
