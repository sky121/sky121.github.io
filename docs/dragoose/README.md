- **DRAGON ART OVERHAUL (2026-07-21):** responds to user feedback ("too cartoony… the dragon should feel realistic, scary, majestic with beautiful animations of flying"). The flat top-down kite rig is rebuilt into an **anatomical membrane-wing dragon** drawn live per frame (one rig used by all five types through their existing `Art.dragonPal` palettes, so identities hold — ember molten, storm slate-blue, verdant mossy, gilded gold, umbra dusk-violet). **Form:** a longer **serpentine body** with a lit **belly keel** and a row of **spine studs**, **scale seams**, a tapering tail; a proper **membrane wing** — an arm + **fanned finger struts** (multiple digits) with the membrane stretched and slightly translucent between them and a **rim-lit leading edge**; a more reptilian **head** (brow ridge, horns/frills) on a sinuous neck. **Shading for depth (not flat):** belly-to-back gradient, darker membrane trailing edge, leading-edge rim light along wing and spine, soft occlusion where wing meets body — painterly/watercolor, menacing and beautiful rather than photoreal. **Flight animation:** a real **wing-beat cycle** (gather → downstroke → glide hold) where the membrane flexes and the finger struts spread/close, the body undulates, and cadence shifts with state (slow majestic glide roaming, harder faster beats enraged/phase-2/dash). **Gary** gets a lighter parallel pass — still a charming goose, a touch more refined with a nicer wing-flap; plume/regalia tint hooks (`Art.goose` `opts.tint`) still apply. Kept ES5, no per-frame allocation beyond the existing scratch pattern, 60fps. Verified via Playwright (390×844): all five dragons render the new rig on-palette at real duel zoom (screenshots judged majestic/menacing vs the old kite), the wing-beat visibly poses across the cycle (frame-diff 218k–267k), each dragon entered + `forceWin` cleanly, Gary's cinder plume tints his coat, zero page errors. *(Salvage-completed: the implementing agent hit its session limit mid-verification with the rig code complete and node --check-passing; the orchestrator ran the full five-dragon + Gary verification and wrote this entry.)*
- **CAMERA + WORLD-SCALE OVERHAUL (2026-07-20):** the fight no longer sits in a fixed VW×VH box — the game now plays in a **world 2.2× the viewport on each axis** (`WORLD_W = 1188`, `WORLD_H = 2112`, from `WORLD_SCALE`) that a **goose-anchored camera with give** frames. This is a *camera & world* change only; the dragon/goose art is untouched (a later task handles art). **World sizes:** both the sky hub and duels use the same 1188×2112 world; the five **realm gates spread across the full world** (same relative layout, scaled to world coords) so travelling between them is real flying (~500–1450 px, ~1–3 s). The duel keeps the dragon roaming a **central arena band** (`arenaCX/CY = W·0.5, H·0.45`; `arenaHW/HH = VW·0.62, VH·0.34`) so it's always circle-able open air but never wanders unreachable — roam targets, dash bounds, lure scatter, and the umbra veil destination are all arena-relative (veil provably lands in-bounds). **Camera design** (`Game.cam`, exposed at `window.__dragoose.camera()`): centre `cx/cy` in world coords tracks the goose through a **critically-damped step** `cam += (target − cam)·(1 − exp(−k·dt))` with **k = 3.6**; a **velocity lead** of **0.42 s** of goose velocity (capped `maxLead = 175 px`) biases the target ahead so you see more sky in the direction of travel — deliberately larger than the follow lag `1/k ≈ 0.278 s` so the net effect is genuinely *ahead* (~+50 px at speed), not cancelled out; a **4 px dead-zone** keeps gentle drift from jittering; the target is **clamped to world bounds** so the viewport never shows void. The goose gets **soft world boundaries** (a gentle inward pull that grows past a 140 px margin + a safety skin clamp — no hard walls, no bounce). **Duel zoom:** the camera eases to **0.62×** on entering a duel (a gentle pull-back; sky is 1.0×), so the same-art dragon reads as **25.3 % of the viewport width vs 40.7 % before** — a creature you circle, not a wall. The Bowing eases the camera gently toward the bowing dragon. **Parallax:** clouds are three layered bands scrolled against the camera centre (far **0.3×**, mid **0.6×**, near/foreground **1.18×**), wrapped in a field `CLOUD_MARGIN` larger than the viewport (fixed pool, cached sprites, zero per-frame alloc). **Off-screen awareness:** soft watercolor **edge chevrons** (`drawEdgeIndicators`/`edgeChevron`) point to the duel dragon in its realm hue (`REALM_COLOR`) and to sky realm gates (fainter, realm-hued) when they leave frame — subtle, on an inset ellipse, never neon. **VW/VH split policy (audited every occurrence):** *world-space* — dragon roam/targeting, realm gate positions, particles, shots, scale drops, gale + spirit, decoys, veil destinations, drift-ring, the daily dashed route path + badges, harmony geese, and floatText over world objects (new `Game.floatWorld` projects world→screen then reuses `floatText`) — all render through **one** `cam.apply` (save/translate/scale/restore) per frame. *Screen-space* — the sky backdrop/sun/haze/blobs, HUD, pause/win/intro overlays, letterbox, vignette, flashes, generic centre-screen hint floatText, the training step prompt (moved to `drawTrainingPrompt`), and the **weather layers** (mist/rain/golden — kept as **viewport atmosphere overlays**, the documented simplest choice: they read as light through the whole frame, so they stay in VW/VH coords, not pinned to a patch of world). Pointer steering projects the goose through the camera so the drag pull-threshold stays in screen px regardless of zoom. **`prefers-reduced-motion`:** camera follow stays (functional) but the velocity lead is dropped (target ≈ goose) and all parallax bands move 1.0× (no differential) for a stable frame. Test hook also gains `world()` and `project(x,y)`. Verified via Playwright (390×844, driven through `window.__dragoose`): world dims 1188×2112 > viewport; goose stays dead-centre while flying; sustained rightward flight puts the camera ahead of the goose (lead) mid-world, and *behind* with no lead under reduced motion; realm-gate edge chevrons when gates off-screen; duel dragon 25.3 % of frame with the ratio stated; dragon-off-screen edge chevron; phase 2 + forceWin in world space; **all five dragons** entered + in-bounds/reachable + forceWin (umbra veil relocation lands in-bounds); five-realm crowning fires; Daily route badges in world space; Fledgling Gale training starts under the camera; weather rain forced; reduced-motion lead-absent + parallax-uniform — normal suite + reduced-motion suite + regression sweep all green, zero page errors.

- **DAILY FLIGHT (2026-07-15):** a date-seeded deterministic challenge run. The `Daily` module (exposed on `window.__dragoose.daily`) builds the day's plan from the **UTC date key** → FNV-1a hash → **mulberry32 PRNG**: a shuffled **realm route** plus ONE modifier from `DAILY_MODS` — *Thin Air* (dodge rests +0.15 s), *Tailwind* (fly speed +12%, via the `Game.flySpeed()` helper), *Brittle Scales* (dragon HP ×0.9, scales ×2), *Long Night* (telegraphs ×0.85) — each applied through `Game.dailyMod(id)` checks at the single point the stat is used. `Game.startRun(true)` sets `Game.daily = {date, route, idx, mod}`; the sky stamps each realm with `routeIdx` and draws **numbered badges** (next stop pulses gold; flown stops fade) plus a faint dashed route path (one shared dash array — no per-frame allocation); `enterRealm` refuses off-route gates with a gentle nudge-back + throttled hint naming the next realm. Retry/restart keep the daily mode; normal runs are untouched (any realm, any order, no modifier, `Game.daily = null`). Relics/scales/plumes bank normally in dailies; the crowning/records fire only if genuinely earned.

- **THE BOWING (2026-07-16):** a short painterly victory moment between the killing blow and the win screen. When a dragon's health reaches zero (normal, ceremonial, or Daily duels alike), the world eases into **slow motion for ~2.2 s of real time**: `Game.bowing` ({t, dur, armed, show}) drives it from `update()`, scaling `dt` by `BOW_SLOWMO` (0.35) for **`updateDragon`/`updateShots`/`Particles.update` only** — the UI, input, background, and Gary himself keep breathing at full speed. The defeated dragon **glides to a gentle stop** (its leftover `vx/vy` decays inside the existing `"bow"` state, which already folds the wings, dips the head, and dissolves into rising gold motes — the flourish choreographs that state rather than inventing a new one), while **22–28 motes of its realm's color drift down like petals** (module-level `BOW_MOTES` map, pre-derived per type: ember rose/cinder, storm rain-light/wisteria, verdant sage/blossom, gilded treasure-light/gold, umbra wisteria/dusk — reusing the pooled `Particles.spawn`, zero per-frame allocation) and `Audio2.bowChord()` hums a soft resolving G-major pad (sines through `tone()`, so it honors mute for free). **Any fresh tap or press skips** straight to the win screen — the skip arms only once the killing-blow press has cleared, and taps during the bow neither dodge nor fire (input queues are consumed; a live charge is quietly released so its drone can't outlive the duel). **Rewards are untouched**: scales/relics/records all bank synchronously in `dragonDefeated` *before* the flourish begins, so skipping (or not) can never double-fire them — the win-screen reveal is just a `showWin` closure handed to `Game.beginBowing()`. **The crowning skips the flourish entirely** (deliberate call: the five-realm finale already owns its golden celebration and 1.4 s clock, and layering a 2.2 s beat in front would delay it — `isFinal` keeps the original `setTimeout` path, byte-for-byte). `prefers-reduced-motion` also bypasses it (the old quick ~600 ms transition, no slow-mo, no petals). Stale-flourish safety: `bowing` is cleared in `startRun` and `toTitle`, and pausing simply freezes the moment (state gate in `update`). The run-stats clock pauses during the bow so the flourish never pads "time in the sky". Test hook: `window.__dragoose.bowing()`. ## How to resume / develop

- **Run locally:** from the repo root, `python3 -m http.server` then open `http://localhost:8000/dragoose.html`. (Must be served over HTTP, not `file://`, so the sprite PNGs load.)
- **Architecture (in `assets/js/dragoose.js`):** single `"use strict"` IIFE, one `<canvas>` at fixed logical resolution **540×960**, letterboxed + DPR-aware (capped 2.5×). Fixed-timestep update (1/60s) with frame clamping + spiral-of-death guard; interpolated render. Modules: **Save** (localStorage), **Audio2** (synthesized Web Audio), **Input** (pointer-primary + keyboard), **Particles** (pool of 260), two **projectile pools** (player 48 / dragon 80), **Pickups**, and a **Game** state machine: `LOADING → TITLE → PLAYING → POWER → PAUSED → DEAD → WIN`. Pauses on tab blur/visibilitychange; honors `prefers-reduced-motion` (tones down shake/flash). Minimal test hook exposed at `window.__dragoose`.
- **Save data:** localStorage key **`dragoose-save`**. Shape e.g. `{"scales":7,"relics":["emberHeart"],"wins":1}`. To reset progress, clear that key in devtools.
- **Editing sprites:** edit the SVGs in `docs/dragoose/art-src/`, then re-render to transparent PNGs with Playwright (`omitBackground:true`, viewport = sprite size, dpr 1) using the included `render.js` / `render-dragons.js` as a starting point, and overwrite the matching file in `images/dragoose/`. Dragons should be quantized (alpha-preserving) to stay ~50–55 KB.
- **Adding a project tile to the Lab** (for future games): copy the `<li class="pool ...">` template documented inside `lab.html` (there's a commented TEMPLATE + "how to add a project" block).

### Controls (implemented)
- **Drag** anywhere = steer (eased toward pointer, momentum + banking tilt).
- **Tap** (<220 ms, no move) = dodge/dash with **0.42 s i-frames** + 0.6 s cooldown (motion streak + shimmer).
- **Hold** (>220 ms) = charge a fireball (arc meter + rising audio + glow); release fires; size/damage scale with charge.
- **Desktop:** mouse drag steers; also WASD/arrows steer, **Space** tap=dodge / hold=charge, **Esc** pause, **M** mute.
- Canvas blocks scroll/zoom/pull-to-refresh (`touch-action: none`).

### Procedural rigged characters (2026-07-08, second pass)
The goose and dragon PNG sprites are **no longer drawn** — both characters are now fully procedural, rigged, and animated in code (`Art` module in `assets/js/dragoose.js`):

- **`Art.goose(ctx, opts)`** — vector goose facing up in a 140-unit box: two-joint wing flap with follow-through (tips lag the stroke, downstroke clamped so the top-down silhouette stays birdlike), banking asymmetry (outer wing extends), teardrop body with feather linework, neck/head/wedge-beak, ember glow in the beak while charging, hurt-tint via color lerp. Flap phase (`p.flapPhase`) accumulates with flight speed and races while charging.
- **`Art.dragon(ctx, opts)`** — vector dragon facing up in a 460-unit box: serpentine 11-segment body drawn as smooth tapered strokes (tail whips with `swayPhase`), swept bat wings with elbow/wrist joints, three membrane fingers and deep scalloped trailing edges that billow in counter-phase, arrow-shaped skull with backswept horns, glowing upswept wedge eyes under hard brow slashes (brighter + veins glow when enraged), spine studs, tail spade. `bow` folds the wings, dips the head and softens the eyes. Palettes for `ember` and `storm` are in `Art.dragonPal` — the storm dragon is a palette swap away.
- Characters render into per-frame **scratch canvases** (`Fx.scratch`) so one `drawImage` alpha fades the whole figure (i-frames, ghosts, bow) without per-path alpha bookkeeping.
- **Perf:** the sky backdrop (gradient, god rays, sun, blooms, haze) paints into a **half-res offscreen layer every other frame** and blits once; the vignette + color grade are one pre-baked layer. Headless-software-rendering benchmark: ~56 fps in phase-2 combat vs ~60 for the pre-revamp build (GPU devices lock at 60). Big previews of the rigs: `window.__dragoose.art` is exposed for test harnesses.
- The old sprite PNGs (`goose.png`, `dragon-*.png`, `fireball.png`, `cloud.png`) remain in the repo but are **no longer preloaded** (2026-07-08) — only the three scale-pickup PNGs load now; everything else is procedural.

## Appendix — original design brief (preserved verbatim from the concept)

### Gameplay
- **No Stats** — equipment, scales, or buffs give an interesting mechanic or ability directly, e.g.:
  - "your next attack burns the enemy"
  - "when hit by an attack reflect damage"
  - "gain movement speed when flying away from enemies"
  - "the higher your movement speed the more your damage"
  - "shoot lightning at enemies when dodging"
- **Open sky (open world).** Dragons each have their own realm you can fly into, which angers that dragon; they then attack you while you're in their realm.
- Equipment changes the goose's sprite look as well as abilities and attacks.
- **No hordes / minions / small basic creatures** to fight — the sky is ruled only by large dragons.
- Since there are no minions, gain experience through **time spent fighting** the dragons. Do enough damage to a dragon and it drops a **scale**, which you collect for more magical powers. The more you fight, the stronger you get until you can defeat all the dragons.
- **One-handed, only thumb, vertical mobile game.** Drag to steer. Tap to dodge/boost. Hold to charge attack.
- **Roguelike** (die → fully restart). But items collected for your **hoard** back at your pond carry to the next goose who takes on the sky.
- Semi-ranged attacks and abilities (fireballs, claw swipe, etc.).
- Defeat a dragon → earn its respect → it gives a **gift from its hoard**. Earn the respect of **all** dragons → you're named **Dragoose**, ruler of the skies; you invite all geese to fly freely; geese and dragons live in harmony.
- **RNG** for rarer gifts from defeating dragons, and rarer scales dropped.
- *Problem:* if you can only defeat a dragon once, what if you want to retry for the rare drops? *Solution:* after earning a dragon's respect, return for a **ceremonial duel**. **Dragons adapt between rematches** — attacks evolve, phases change, gain counters to your build, become more aggressive.
- **Rare drops change playstyle/aesthetics, not power.** First victory guaranteed (respect, story progression, signature power); repeat victories: chance for rare scales, alternate gifts, cosmetic gear, legendary mutations. Possibly higher drop rate for faster time-to-victory.

### Backstory
Dragoose is the story of **Gary**, a goose who dreams of flying among the clouds — but the skies belong to dragons. For generations, geese have lived in fear, hiding on the ground while dragons soar above; to dragons, geese are prey.

One day Gary discovers a fallen dragon scale glowing with strange magical energy. Drawn to it, he reaches out — a torrent of magic erupts, and everything goes dark. When he awakens, he can **breathe fire**. Believing this power may finally earn him a place in the skies, Gary takes flight for the first time — unaware the dragons may never accept a goose among them.

As Gary defeats each dragon, he earns their respect in battle. Rather than dying, the dragons acknowledge his strength and offer a gift from their hoard — relics infused with ancient draconic magic. With every victory he grows stronger, and the dragons begin to see him not as prey but as one of their own.

After earning the respect of every dragon, Gary is given a new title: **Dragoose** — the first goose accepted among dragonkind. He unites the skies and the earth; geese are finally free to fly alongside dragons, and the skies enter a new age of harmony.
